# Structural model of H5 HA bound to tufted duck MHC-II

We have used deep mutational scanning data in combination with Cryo-EM to create a structural model of H5 HA bound to tufted duck MHC-II. Using H5 pseudovirus-based deep mutational scanning we made HA variant with better binding to tufted duck MHC-II. Then we used this variant to solve a 4.8 Å Cryo-EM structure of HA bound to MHC-II. Because at this resolution we cannot resolve residue-level interactions, we rigid-body fit into the cryoEM model a previously published high-resolution cryo-EM structure of H5 HA (PDB: 9DWE) and an AlphaFold 3-predicted structure of tufted duck MHC-II, and then locally relaxed the models into the cryo-EM density using ISOLDE. 

Below is our proposed structural model of H5 HA bound to tufted duck MHC-II. HA in this structure is colored by mutation effects on MHC-II binding while MHC-II is colored by mutation effects on HA binding. In both cases red indicated sites where mutations mostly decrease binding and blue indicated sites where mutations mostly increase binding. Data for tufted duck MHC-II deep mutational scanning is available [here](https://dms-vep.org/Tufted-duck-MHCII-DMS/) Structure visualization was generated using [ChimeraX](https://www.cgl.ucsf.edu/chimerax/).
